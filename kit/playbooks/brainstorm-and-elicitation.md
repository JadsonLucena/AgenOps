# Brainstorm and Deep Elicitation Playbook

## Purpose

Turn ambiguous intent into a shared problem frame through deep brainstorming and elicitation before design or implementation begins.

## Use when

- requirements are incomplete, contradictory, or implied
- the problem domain is new or high risk
- there are many unknowns, hidden assumptions, or policy constraints
- a change needs a small, reviewable first slice

## Steps

1. Run a structured brainstorm before any design or implementation work.
2. Turn ambiguous intent into a shared problem frame through deep brainstorming and elicitation.
3. Separate facts, assumptions, constraints, risks, and open questions.
4. Ask the smallest useful question set until the gaps are reduced.
5. Capture success criteria, business rules, and non-functional needs.
6. Identify whether the next step is a spec document design or a direct small-scope update.
7. Propose candidate design chunks small enough to be reviewed independently.
8. Summarize open questions and the decision rationale.

## Outputs

- brainstorm brief
- question backlog
- assumptions log
- constraints map
- problem framing
- spec design prompt
- candidate design chunks
- traceability anchors

## Guardrails

- Do not jump to code before the problem is framed.
- Do not hide unresolved gaps.
- Keep the output short enough to reuse in downstream docs and planning.

# Budget Policy

## Rules

### 1. Every meaningful workflow has a budget
If the workflow can scale, it needs a cost envelope.

### 2. Budgets have soft and hard limits
- soft limit: warn and investigate
- hard limit: stop or escalate

### 3. Budgets are tied to scopes
A budget belongs to a task, workflow, team, project, or environment.

### 4. Costs must be measured against value units
Do not track spend only in aggregate. Track it against the unit of value being delivered.

### 5. Hidden cost matters
Loops, retries, extra reviews, and long context chains are real cost drivers.

### 6. Exceptions must be approved
Budget overrides require explicit approval and evidence.

### 7. Drift triggers cost review
If planning and execution diverge materially, the cost envelope is likely being consumed inefficiently.

### 8. Budgets are part of governance
Economic control is a governance concern, not merely a finance concern.
